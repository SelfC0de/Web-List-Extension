Privacy Policy for Ask the Network

Last Updated: March 14, 2024

Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Ask the Network ("we", "our", or "the extension") handles your privacy and data protection rights.

Data Collection

Ask the Network is designed with privacy in mind and operates with minimal data requirements. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Specifically:

  • We do not collect any personal information
  • We do not track your browsing history
  • We do not monitor your online activities
  • We do not store any user data on external servers

Local Storage

The extension uses Chrome's local storage API to save your preferences (selected operating system and browser configurations). This data:

  • Is stored locally on your device only
  • Is never transmitted to external servers
  • Can be cleared by uninstalling the extension

Permissions

The extension requires certain permissions to function:

  • declarativeNetRequest: To modify User-Agent headers
  • storage: To save your preferences locally
  • tabs: To open new tabs with modified settings
  • host_permissions: To modify User-Agent for websites you visit

Third-Party Services

The extension includes a link to whatismybrowser.com for browser information checking. When you use this feature:

  • You will be directed to their website
  • Their privacy policy will apply to your interaction with their service
  • We do not share any data with them

Data Security

Since we don't collect any personal data, there is no risk of data breaches or unauthorized access to your personal information through our extension.

Changes to Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will post any changes on this page.
